# Deep Dives

Optional extensions to the core crash course. Each module is self-contained — pick the ones that match your interests and skip the rest.

---

## How This Course Is Organized

This crash course uses a three-tier structure:

**Tier 1: The Crash Course (Weeks 1-4)** — The core curriculum. Conceptual fluency, hands-on AI tool exercises, career positioning, and starting a collaboration. Everyone should complete this. **No coding required.**

**Tier 2: Deep Dives (This Section)** — Optional extensions that go deeper on specific topics. Pick the ones relevant to your interests and needs. Each is self-contained. **R notebooks require R/RStudio.**

**Tier 3: Continued Learning** — The reading list, methods primers, courses, and communities in [Reading List](../supplementary/reading-list.md). Your ongoing development path after the crash course.

## How to Use These Modules

- **They are independent.** You can do one, several, or all of them in any order, as long as you meet the listed prerequisites.
- **They build on the crash course, not on each other.** Prerequisites always point back to a specific week of the core curriculum, not to other deep dives.
- **Each module assumes you have completed the prerequisite material.** If a concept feels unfamiliar, revisit the relevant week first.

---

## What These Are Not

These deep dives are not a second course. They do not attempt to cover every topic in machine learning. They exist to give you slightly more depth on the specific topics that psychology researchers ask about most often.

If you finish these and want to keep going, see [Reading List](../supplementary/reading-list.md) for textbooks, courses, communities, and methods primers that pick up where this crash course leaves off.
