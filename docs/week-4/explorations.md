Two activities that turn your knowledge into interview-ready confidence. Both are core.

## Exploration 1: Interview Practice

**Tools needed**: ChatGPT or Claude, your voice
**Goal**: Practice answering AI/ML interview questions out loud, then refine with AI feedback

!!! example "This is your Week 4 starting point"
    If you are coming from the guide, you were sent here first. Complete this activity before reading the rest of Week 4.

### Part A: Answer Out Loud

Pick 3 of the questions below and answer them **out loud** — not written. Record yourself on your phone, or practice with a friend. Speaking forces you to find the words you actually have, not the ones you wish you had.

- "How does your research connect to AI and machine learning?"
- "What experience do you have with machine learning?"
- "What specific AI/ML project would you pursue first?"
- "What ethical concerns do you see with using AI in your research?"
- "How would you collaborate with a data science center?"
- "What's the difference between what you'd do with ML versus traditional statistics?"

### Part B: Get AI Feedback

Pick the answer you felt least confident about. Paste it into ChatGPT or Claude with this prompt:

```text
I'm preparing for a faculty interview in psychology. Here is my answer to an interview question about AI/ML. Critique it: Is it honest about my skill level? Does it show genuine understanding? Does it overstate or understate anything? Give me 3 specific improvements, then write a revised version.

The question was: [QUESTION]
My answer: [YOUR ANSWER]
```

### Part C: Write Your Final Version

Compare the AI revision to your original. What did it improve? What felt less authentic? Write your final version — one that's both polished and sounds like you.

## Exploration 2: Build Your Elevator Pitch

**Tools needed**: ChatGPT or Claude, your voice
**Goal**: Write and practice a 60-second pitch for a casual conference setting

### Part A: Draft It

Write a 60-second pitch using this structure:

> "I study [X]. My data includes [Y]. I'm excited about [Z computational direction]. My role is [domain expert angle]."

Keep it under 100 words. It should feel natural to say at a conference reception, not like you're reading a research statement.

### Part B: Get AI Feedback

Paste your draft into ChatGPT or Claude:

```text
I'm a psychology PhD preparing for the academic job market. Here's my 60-second elevator pitch that includes my AI/ML direction. Critique it: Does it sound natural? Is the AI/ML part honest and specific? Does it position me as a domain expert, not an AI expert? Give me 3 improvements, then write a revised version.

My pitch: [YOUR PITCH]
```

### Part C: Practice It

Say your final version out loud 3 times. Not reading — from memory. It's okay if it's slightly different each time. Save the written version somewhere you can find it before your next conference or interview.
