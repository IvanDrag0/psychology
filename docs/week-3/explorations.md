## Exploration 1: Draft Your 1-Page Research Note

!!! success "Deliverable"
    This exploration produces the course's main tangible output: a 1-page research note connecting AI/ML to your work. You'll draft, critique, and refine it through an iterative process with AI assistance.

**Tools needed**: ChatGPT or Claude
**Goal**: Draft, critique, and refine a research note that positions your work at the intersection of your domain expertise and AI/ML methods

### Background

A 1-page research note serves multiple purposes: it's raw material for your research statement, a conversation starter with potential collaborators, and a thinking exercise that forces you to articulate the connection between your work and computational methods. The iterative draft → critique → revise process is itself a skill you'll use in all AI-assisted writing.

### Instructions

#### Step 1: Generate a Draft

Open ChatGPT or Claude and paste this prompt:

```text
I'm a psychology PhD preparing for the academic job market. My research focuses on [YOUR RESEARCH AREA — e.g., body image, mother-daughter relationships, and adolescent development]. I study [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF YOUR WORK]. My methods include [YOUR METHODS — e.g., longitudinal studies, qualitative interviews, survey research, quantitative analysis in R].

I'm learning to integrate AI/ML into my research program. Specifically, I'm interested in:
1. Using NLP to analyze text data (e.g., conversation transcripts, social media posts) at scale
2. Building predictive models that integrate multiple risk factors
3. Using AI tools for text classification and qualitative coding
4. Ethical considerations of AI/ML tools applied to my populations

Write a 1-page research note (approximately 400-500 words) that:
- Opens with the core research question my work addresses
- Briefly describes my established findings (2-3 sentences)
- Bridges naturally to how AI/ML extends this work
- Describes 2-3 specific computational directions I plan to pursue
- Closes with why this matters (clinical impact, scientific contribution)
- Sounds like a confident early-career researcher, not a seasoned AI expert
- Frames AI/ML as a natural extension of my existing work, not a separate interest
```

Read the output carefully. Does it sound like you? Does anything feel overstated or understated?

#### Step 2: Critique and Revise

Paste this follow-up:

```text
Now critique that research note as if you were a psychology search committee member. What's strong? What's weak? Does anything sound overstated for someone early in their AI/ML journey? Does it clearly communicate what I would ACTUALLY DO in my first few years? Give me 3 specific improvements, then write a revised version that incorporates them.
```

Read the critique and the revision. Edit the revised version so it sounds like you — add your own findings, your specific population, and details that make it authentic.

#### Step 3: Save Your Work

Save the final revised research note. This is raw material for your research statement, cover letters, and interview talking points — not a finished product, but a strong starting point you can refine for specific positions.

> Compare your note with the three research statement variants on the [Templates](templates.md) page. Grab any language that fits.

## Exploration 2: Grant Angle Generator

!!! tip "If you have more time"
    This exploration is optional but highly recommended if you're preparing for interviews. The outputs are directly useful when asked "What would your first grant be?"

**Tools needed**: ChatGPT or Claude
**Goal**: Brainstorm grant-fundable research ideas that combine your expertise with AI/ML, and draft a preliminary R21 project summary

### Instructions

#### Step 1: Brainstorm Grant Ideas

```text
I'm a psychology researcher planning my early-career grant strategy. My research areas are:
- [YOUR AREA 1]
- [YOUR AREA 2]
- [YOUR AREA 3]

I want to write grants that combine my psychology expertise with AI/ML methods. For each of the following funding mechanisms, suggest 2 specific project ideas. For each: (1) project title, (2) 2-sentence summary, (3) AI/ML method(s) involved, (4) why it's innovative, (5) which mechanism fits best.

Funding mechanisms: NIH R21 (exploratory, 2 years, ~$275K), NIH R01 (major, 5 years, ~$1.25M), NIH K-award (career development, 3-5 years), NSF CAREER (early career, 5 years).
```

#### Step 2: Develop One Idea

Choose the idea that excites you most and paste this follow-up:

```text
Let's develop idea [number] further. Write a 200-word project summary suitable for an NIH R21. Include: significance, innovation, 2-3 specific aims, and anticipated impact. Then critique it as an NIH reviewer — is the scope right? Are the aims achievable in 2 years? What's the weakest point? Write a revised version addressing the weaknesses.
```

Save your brainstormed ideas and the revised project summary. These are interview gold — when a search committee member asks "What would you pursue with start-up funding?" you'll have specific, well-developed answers.
