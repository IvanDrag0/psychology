## Research Statement Templates

=== "Variant 1: Emerging AI/ML"

    Best for positions that mention AI/ML as "preferred" or "desired" — not required. This framing emphasizes your established expertise and positions AI/ML as the natural next step.

    > My research program examines [your primary research focus, e.g., "how maternal communication about bodies and appearance shapes adolescent body image and eating disorder risk"]. Through [brief description of your work, e.g., "longitudinal studies and qualitative analysis of mother-daughter interactions"], I have established that [your key findings, e.g., "specific patterns of maternal body talk — particularly appearance-focused comments directed at daughters — predict body dissatisfaction trajectories across adolescence, even after controlling for peer and media influences"].
    >
    > This work has generated rich, multivariate datasets and detailed qualitative data that are ideally suited to computational approaches. My next phase of research integrates natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning to extend these findings in three ways. First, I will use NLP to analyze large corpora of parent-child conversation data, identifying linguistic markers of risk at a scale and granularity that manual qualitative coding cannot achieve. Second, I am developing machine learning models that combine parental, peer, and media factors to predict eating disorder risk trajectories — moving beyond individual risk factors to integrative, person-centered prediction. Third, I plan to leverage digital phenotyping approaches to capture real-time relationships between social media exposure and body image fluctuations in adolescent samples.
    >
    > I am building these computational skills through training in AI tools for text analysis and classification and engaging with the growing literature on computational approaches to clinical and developmental psychology. I am eager to deepen this expertise through collaboration with computational researchers and access to [institutional resources, e.g., "the university's data science center and computing infrastructure"]. My goal is not to become a data scientist — it is to be the psychologist who ensures that AI/ML tools applied to body image and adolescent development are theoretically grounded, clinically relevant, and ethically responsible.

=== "Variant 2: Interdisciplinary Bridge"

    Best for positions at universities with existing data science or computational research infrastructure. This framing emphasizes collaboration and what you uniquely bring.

    > My research sits at a productive intersection: I study [your research focus] using methods rooted in clinical and developmental science, and I am increasingly engaged with the computational tools that can amplify this work's impact. I see my role not as a computer scientist who happens to study psychology, but as a domain expert who brings irreplaceable knowledge to interdisciplinary AI/ML teams.
    >
    > In my work on [specific research area, e.g., "mother-daughter body image communication and adolescent eating disorder risk"], I have developed deep expertise in the psychological constructs, developmental processes, and clinical implications that computational tools must be grounded in to be meaningful. I understand what "body dissatisfaction" means clinically — not just statistically. I know which qualitative distinctions matter when NLP models classify body-related language. I can evaluate whether an ML model's predictions are psychologically plausible, not just mathematically accurate.
    >
    > What I am building toward is a collaborative research model: I design the studies, define the constructs, and interpret the findings; computational collaborators contribute methodological expertise in NLP, predictive modeling, and digital phenotyping. Together, we can address questions neither of us could alone — such as identifying real-time linguistic markers in parent-child communication that predict eating disorder onset, or building predictive models that personalize prevention for adolescents based on their unique risk profiles. I have begun developing hands-on experience with AI tools for text analysis and classification and am eager to grow these through the interdisciplinary resources at [institution].

=== "Variant 3: Ethics-Forward"

    Best for positions that emphasize responsible AI, health equity, or work with vulnerable populations. This framing leads with the ethical dimension as a scholarly contribution.

    > As AI and machine learning tools are increasingly applied to mental health research and clinical practice, there is an urgent need for researchers who understand both the technical capabilities and the human stakes. My research on [your focus area] positions me to contribute to this critical intersection — particularly regarding how AI/ML tools interact with body image, appearance, and weight in the lives of adolescents.
    >
    > My scholarly concern is that AI/ML systems developed without deep psychological and developmental expertise risk perpetuating the very harms we study. Body image classification models trained predominantly on thin, White beauty standards will encode those standards algorithmically. Eating disorder screening tools calibrated to stereotypical presentations will miss the diverse ways this pathology manifests. Predictive models that use BMI as a primary feature risk reinforcing weight stigma. These are not hypothetical concerns — they are active problems as AI tools enter clinical and educational spaces that serve youth.
    >
    > I am developing a research program that integrates AI/ML methods with a commitment to responsible, equitable application. This includes [specific plans: NLP analysis of body-related discourse with attention to cultural and linguistic diversity, predictive models validated across demographic groups, participatory approaches that involve adolescents and families in AI tool development]. I bring to this work both hands-on experience with AI-assisted text classification and qualitative coding and a clinical-developmental lens that centers the wellbeing of the young people these tools are designed to serve.

I am trained in the design and analysis of studies with adolescent populations and parent-child dyads, and I am building toward integrating NLP for text analysis and predictive modeling into my methodological toolkit. I value both rigor and interpretability — my goal is models that are not only accurate but clinically meaningful.

## Teaching Statement Language

### Course Development Ideas

> I am prepared to teach core courses in [your areas: developmental psychology, clinical psychology, research methods] and am excited about developing new courses that reflect the evolving methodological landscape. Specifically, I envision:
>
> - **"Psychology in the Age of AI"** (undergraduate): An introduction to how AI and machine learning are transforming psychological research and practice, covering applications from NLP-based therapy analysis to predictive models for mental health outcomes. No programming required — the emphasis is on critical thinking about AI's role in understanding human behavior.
>
> - **"Computational Methods for Psychology Researchers"** (graduate): A hands-on introduction to AI-assisted text analysis and computational methods, designed for students with quantitative backgrounds but no programming experience. Covers supervised and unsupervised learning, NLP basics, and predictive modeling — all with psychology-relevant datasets and questions.
>
> - **"Ethics of AI in Mental Health"** (graduate seminar): A seminar examining the ethical, legal, and clinical implications of AI-assisted assessment, intervention, and research — with special attention to vulnerable populations including children and adolescents.

### Mentoring Computational Students

> I am committed to mentoring a new generation of psychologists who are fluent in both psychological theory and computational methods. My mentoring approach emphasizes that strong AI/ML work in psychology requires deep domain expertise — not just technical skill. I would support graduate students in developing interdisciplinary collaborations, attending computational methods workshops, and building portfolios that demonstrate both psychological insight and methodological innovation.

## Diversity Statement Connection

### AI Ethics and Equity Angle

> My commitment to diversity, equity, and inclusion extends to my research methodology. As AI and machine learning tools are increasingly used in psychological assessment, prediction, and intervention, ensuring that these tools serve diverse populations equitably is both an ethical imperative and a scholarly challenge. My research engages directly with this issue: I study body image in adolescent populations, a domain where algorithmic bias is particularly consequential. AI models trained on narrow data — predominantly thin, White, cisgender samples — risk encoding culturally specific beauty standards as universal norms, producing tools that misclassify, underserve, or actively harm diverse communities.
>
> I am committed to developing and validating AI/ML tools that work across racial, ethnic, body-size, and gender-diverse populations. This includes ensuring diverse training data, validating model performance across demographic subgroups, and involving community members in the design and evaluation of computational tools. I see responsible AI in psychology as fundamentally a diversity and equity issue — and I bring both the methodological training and the scholarly commitment to address it.

## Job Talk Slide Suggestions

You don't need to restructure your entire job talk. Add AI/ML strategically:

### Slide: Future Directions

**Title**: "Where This Program Is Going: Computational Approaches to [Your Area]"

Suggested content (3-4 bullet points):

- **NLP for scaled qualitative analysis**: "Applying natural language processing to analyze mother-daughter communication about bodies across large, diverse samples — extending my qualitative work computationally"
- **Predictive modeling for risk identification**: "Building machine learning models that integrate parental, peer, and media risk factors to predict eating disorder trajectories — enabling personalized prevention"
- **Digital phenotyping**: "Leveraging smartphone-based passive sensing and ecological momentary assessment to capture real-time body image dynamics in adolescents"
- **Ethical AI for youth populations**: "Ensuring that AI tools applied to adolescent body image and eating disorders are validated across diverse populations and free from weight-based algorithmic bias"

### Slide: Methods (if you have one)

Add a brief note to your existing methods description:

> "My future work will extend these qualitative and longitudinal approaches with computational methods — particularly NLP for automated text analysis and machine learning for predictive modeling. I'm building these skills through hands-on AI tool experience and computational methods training and plan to develop them further through collaboration with [type of collaborator]."

### General Advice

- Keep AI/ML to 1-2 slides maximum in a standard job talk
- Let it emerge naturally from your established work — don't front-load it
- Have backup slides with more detail in case of questions
- Practice answering "Can you say more about the ML component?" conversationally
