## 1. Reading the Job Market

### What Search Committees Really Mean

When a job posting says "experience with AI/ML preferred," what do they actually want?

**The Spectrum of Expectations**:

| Posting Language | What They Probably Want | Your Position |
|-----------------|------------------------|---------------|
| "Familiarity with AI/ML" | Conceptual literacy, ability to collaborate | **You're ready now** |
| "Interest in computational approaches" | A research vision that includes AI/ML | **You're ready now** |
| "Experience with machine learning preferred" | Some hands-on experience, even basic | **You're nearly there** (AI tool exercises + continued learning) |
| "Strong computational skills required" | Active use of ML in research program | **Stretch position** — frame as growth trajectory |
| "Expertise in deep learning/NLP required" | This is a computational methods hire | **Not the right fit** (and that's fine) |

The vast majority of clinical/developmental psychology positions that mention AI/ML fall in the first three categories. They want someone who can bridge domains, not a computer scientist.

### The Competitive Advantage Frame

**Most applicants either have deep AI/ML skills but lack domain expertise, or have deep domain expertise but can't speak AI/ML at all.**

You are positioning yourself in the high-value middle ground:

- Deep domain expertise in your research area
- Methodological fluency in quantitative analysis
- Emerging AI/ML literacy with hands-on experience using AI tools
- Understanding of ethical implications of AI/ML in your populations

This profile is rarer and more valuable than you think.

## 2. Writing a Research Statement That Incorporates AI/ML

### The Strategic Framework

Your research statement should follow a **past → present → future** arc where AI/ML enters naturally as the future direction:

1. **Established program** (past/present): Your research expertise. This is the foundation — don't minimize it
2. **Bridge** (present): How your existing methods and questions naturally lead to computational approaches
3. **Vision** (future): Specific AI/ML directions you plan to pursue, framed as extensions of your current work

### How to Frame Existing Work as Foundation

**Instead of**: "I plan to learn machine learning and apply it to my research."

**Try**: "My program of research has generated the kind of rich, multivariate longitudinal data that is ideally suited to machine learning approaches — particularly predictive modeling and natural language processing."

**Instead of**: "I'm interested in AI but don't have much experience with it yet."

**Try**: "Having established the theoretical and empirical foundations of [your research area], I am now positioned to extend this work using computational methods that can capture the complexity of these processes at scale."

### Red Flags to Avoid

Search committees will notice if you:

- **Overstate your expertise**: Don't claim skills you don't have
- **Treat AI/ML as separate from your work**: It should feel like a natural next step, not a bolt-on
- **Name-drop without understanding**: Mentioning "transformer architectures" without being able to explain them signals superficial engagement
- **Ignore ethics**: Any serious AI/ML research program acknowledges ethical dimensions
- **Promise too much**: "I will explore how NLP can scale my qualitative research" is credible. "I will develop a novel AI platform" is not

## 3. Grant Angles

### NIH Funding Opportunities

Your research + AI/ML creates several fundable angles:

#### R21 (Exploratory/Developmental)

Ideal for AI/ML-adjacent work — designed for exploratory studies and new methodological directions (2 years, ~$275K total).

- **NLP Analysis angle**: Use NLP to analyze transcripts or text data, identify linguistic predictors of outcomes
- **Predictive Modeling angle**: Build ML models combining multiple risk factors, moving beyond individual predictors
- **Digital Phenotyping angle**: Combine passive smartphone monitoring with EMA, use ML to identify person-specific triggers

#### R01 (Major Research)

AI/ML components strengthen the "Innovation" section:

- "To our knowledge, this will be the first study to apply [method] to [your data type]"
- "Machine learning approaches can identify complex, nonlinear combinations of risk and protective factors that traditional analysis misses"

#### K-Award (Career Development)

K awards include a training component — perfect for your situation:

- Frame career development around acquiring AI/ML skills through workshops, coursework, and mentored collaboration
- Identify a computational mentor
- Frame the research project as applying newly acquired skills to established questions

### Writing the "Innovation" Section

Key phrases:

- "This study is among the first to apply [method] to [your research area]"
- "NLP allows examination of [construct] at a scale and granularity not possible with manual coding"
- "The integration of [AI/ML approach] with [your established method] represents a methodological advance"

## 4. Interview Preparation

### Common Questions with Answer Frameworks

For detailed practice questions with model answers, see [Interview Prep Guide](../supplementary/interview-prep-guide.md).

#### "Tell us about your research program and where it's headed."

**Framework**: Past accomplishment → natural bridge → AI/ML vision → practical next steps

#### "What experience do you have with AI or machine learning?"

**Framework**: Honest + strategic. Lead with conceptual understanding, follow with hands-on AI tool experience, close with vision and collaboration plans.

#### "How would you collaborate with our data science center?"

**Framework**: I bring X (domain expertise, data, research design, clinical interpretation), they bring Y (methods expertise, coding, optimization), together we do Z (work that's both technically sound and clinically meaningful).

#### "What's your biggest methodological gap?"

**Framework**: Honesty is a strength. Name it, show what you're doing about it, frame it as growth.

### Questions to Ask Them

- "What resources does the university offer for faculty learning computational methods?"
- "Are there faculty in other departments working on NLP or ML who might collaborate?"
- "Does the university have a data science center that supports social science research?"
- "How does the department see the role of AI in psychology research going forward?"

## 5. Understanding Institutional AI/ML Resources

### What to Look for During Campus Visits

**Computing Infrastructure**: HPC clusters, cloud computing support, software licenses

**People and Centers**: Data science centers, computational faculty, interdisciplinary teams

**Training and Support**: Workshops, boot camps, consulting services

**Funding Infrastructure**: Seed funding, internal grants for computational or interdisciplinary work
